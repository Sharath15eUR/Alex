1. 802.11a
Frequency: 5 GHz band
Modulation: Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing (OFDM)
Maximum Data Rate: 54 Mbps
Range: Shorter range due to higher frequency

Characteristics:
Less interference due to the 5 GHz band.
Primarily used in high-density environments like offices.
No backward compatibility with 802.11b/g (2.4 GHz band).

2. 802.11b
Frequency: 2.4 GHz band
Modulation: Complementary Code Keying (CCK)
Maximum Data Rate: 11 Mbps
Range: Longer range compared to 802.11a (due to lower frequency)

Characteristics:
Lower speed and less efficient compared to 802.11a.
Prone to interference from devices like microwaves and Bluetooth.
More range but less data rate and efficiency.

3. 802.11g
Frequency: 2.4 GHz band
Modulation: OFDM
Maximum Data Rate: 54 Mbps
Range: Similar to 802.11b (longer range than 802.11a)

Characteristics:
Backward compatible with 802.11b.
Improved data rate and efficiency compared to 802.11b.
Prone to interference from other devices in the 2.4 GHz range.

4. 802.11n (Wi-Fi 4)
Frequency: 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands
Modulation: OFDM, MIMO (Multiple Input Multiple Output)
Maximum Data Rate: 600 Mbps (with 4 spatial streams)
Range: Improved range due to MIMO technology

Characteristics:
MIMO technology enables the use of multiple antennas to increase throughput.
Dual-band operation allows use of both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands.
Improved reliability and performance in environments with interference.
Backward compatible with 802.11a/b/g.

5. 802.11ac (Wi-Fi 5)
Frequency: 5 GHz band
Modulation: OFDM, 256-QAM (Quadrature Amplitude Modulation), MU-MIMO (Multi-User MIMO)
Maximum Data Rate: 1.3 Gbps (with 3 spatial streams) up to 3.5 Gbps (with 8 spatial streams)
Range: Similar to 802.11n but better performance at longer ranges due to advanced modulation techniques

Characteristics:
MU-MIMO allows multiple devices to communicate simultaneously, improving efficiency.
Wider channel bandwidth (up to 160 MHz).
Higher throughput due to 256-QAM.
Backward compatible with 802.11n.
Focuses on the 5 GHz band, minimizing interference.

6. 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6)
Frequency: 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz (future support for 6 GHz with Wi-Fi 6E)
Modulation: OFDMA (Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access), 1024-QAM, MU-MIMO
Maximum Data Rate: 9.6 Gbps (with 8 spatial streams)
Range: Improved range with more efficient spectrum usage and better handling of interference

Characteristics:
OFDMA allows more efficient spectrum usage by subdividing channels and serving multiple devices simultaneously.
1024-QAM increases throughput.
Target Wake Time (TWT) improves battery life for IoT devices.
MU-MIMO has been enhanced to support uplink as well as downlink communication.
Wi-Fi 6E introduces the 6 GHz band, increasing available spectrum.
Backward compatible with 802.11a/b/g/n/ac.

7. 802.11be (Wi-Fi 7) (under development, expected release 2024)
Frequency: 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, and 6 GHz bands
Modulation: 4096-QAM, MU-MIMO, OFDMA
Maximum Data Rate: Up to 46 Gbps
Range: Further improvements due to higher-order modulation

Characteristics:
Massive MIMO (up to 16 streams) to boost throughput.
Channel bonding of up to 320 MHz channels.
Higher-order modulation (4096-QAM) for higher throughput.
Enhanced performance in dense environments (offices, stadiums, etc.)
Supports 6 GHz for more spectrum.
